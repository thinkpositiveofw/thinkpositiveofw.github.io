RICH NOW, POOR LATER
(THE OFW CYCLE)

For the past four decades, Filipinos have been leaving the country in the hope of finding greener pastures abroad. Over the years, the pursuit of a better income and better opportunities has compelled a lot of Filipinos to word or migrate overseas to provide a more secure future for their children by ensuring theireducation, or simply a more comfortable life for their families

                                  -credit- to Terence Rey Lim & Tet Cudiamat-lim
                                  (The OFW FRICH handbook)

This is where the problem lies. while there is a lot of overseas money flowing into the philippines, a big chunk of it is being spent on consumption or on liebilities rather than on assets, investments, or business.

Always complaining that she’s broke, she spends money as fast as she gets it – often using her credit cards to fund her unsustainable lifestyle.

Rather than analysing her obligations and needs for the month before spending any of it, she spends her entire paycheque first on whatever she wants, leaving her broke for much of the time between pay periods..

Twenty years will pass swiftly and the OFW is still abroad, with little or nothing saved. The kids have grown and are about to graduate. There are still no jobs at home. The jobs that are available don't pay very much. could they start business? but the perents don't have the money for that. There is still no future for them here, so where do they go? Abroad, like their parents did before them. And a new OFW cycle begins.
